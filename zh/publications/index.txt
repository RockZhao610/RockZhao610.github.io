16:T58f,Compound limb motor imagery (MI) paradigms, involving coordinated upper and lower limb imagery, align naturally with human walking pattern and have demonstrated relatively high decoding accuracy in offline electroencephalography (EEG)-based brain-computer interface (BCI) studies. However, their online implementation remains largely unexplored due to practical challenges. In this study, we propose an online compound limb-MI-BCI walking system based on a deep learning model and a shared control strategy. We first evaluate three common deep learning models (EEGNet, DeepConvNet, and ShallowConvNet) for decoding performance without complex preprocessing or handcrafted features. Offline results from 11 participants show that all models achieve accuracies significantly above chance, with EEGNet achieving the highest accuracy (70.76 ± 8.50%). Building on this, we employ EEGNet with transfer learning for decoding, and a shared control strategy based on majority voting and prior knowledge to enhance walking control in the online system. Online experiments with 8 participants demonstrate feasibility, yielding an average decoding accuracy of 63.54 ± 6.83%, a task completion rate of 89.17 ± 3.15% with an averaged step frequency of 13.74 ± 0.27 steps per minute.
